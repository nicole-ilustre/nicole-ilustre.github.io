    Career Pathway

    When I first decided to learn computer programming, I wasn't fully convinced that it would be something I would enjoy or excel in which is one of the biggest reason why I decided to do a bootcamp instead of a full university degree. I do envision myself building a successful career in the tech industry, although I am not 100% certain yet what exactly I want to be doing. So I plan to apply the lean start up approach. I am going to learn the basics, become a web developer, see how I go from there. While I'm doing that, I plan to study and learn more about the different languages and hopefully that will eventually lead me to what I really want to focus on and what I really want to achieve.

    Strength and Limitations

    Self-sufficient
    Strength Limitation Solution
    As long as I have the goal in mind or I know what the objective is, I am motivated to achieve them on my own and can find ways to reach the objective by myself. But this can also sometimes be my weakness. Most of the time, I really don't feel comfortable asking for help. I do not like the feeling of disturbing others and on the other hand, I also do not like the feeling of depending on others. I feel helpless. I will work on finding the balance between both. Usually, I try to do everything I can to find the solution to a problem or to do a task on my own until I have exhausted all my efforts and if that doesn't yield anything, I will have to muster the confidence and as much as possible, write down the problem and the question. This way it will be easier for me to appraoch people for help.
    Perfectionist/Obsessive
    Strength Limitation Solution
    This is mostly a strength for me as it helps me achieve things. I get obsessive over a lot of things. Playing games, solving a puzzle, solving a problem. I wouldn't stop until I've done things perfectly. A perfect example is my obsessiveness to get all the stars or to get a perfect score in a game and I will keep playing over and over again until I get it. Obviously, this drive helps me get things done. But sometimes, I find it hard to let go and end up spending way too much time on one small thing. It also affects my mood sometimes as I get upset or be really distracted because I would keep thinking about that one thing. I will learn to reassess a problem and determine how big it is and how much effort and time should I be putting on it. I have to learn to stick to a timeline and not deviate too much.
    Kinesthetic Learner
    Strength Limitation Solution
    I think this is a strength for me with programming as it is a very hands-on job. I learn most when I am the one typing and doing the activity. This can sometimes be a limitation as there are times (there were times in foundations) where we needed to watch a video or we needed to read articles and it was hard for me to keep focused and not get distracted. I usually take notes or code along with the video. That way I am physically doing it as well. I think this is the best way for me to learn.

    Workload

    Since the beginning of foundations, I have thoroughly planned my schedule, daily and hourly. This way I feel like I have things controlled and I know I am achieving what I need to achieve in the day. I have also allocated extra time just in case I do end up needing more time on a specific project. As long as I keep to my schedule, I think everything will be under control. But when things do get to me, I usually channel my frustration somewhere else. I train aerials and circus a lot as a hobby. I have aslo planned my training schedule around studying. So if ever I do get frustrated, I will step away for a little bit and train and usually that does help me recover.

    Expectations from facilitations team

    I am such a why person and I need to know the end goal and the objective. I expect that all project objectives are communicated well and all expectations from us are also well communicated. I expect to be shown the answer to any of my questions by explaining it and going through it together and not just answered with a short answer. I expect them to be patient and understanding as I am sure we will have our moments of struggle and frustration.

    Schedule

    Besides studying full-time, I am also prioritising my own personal trainingas this really helps me focus on studying and helps me deal with frustrations and distractions. I normal train at night either between 5:30-7:30 or 6:30-7:30. I also plan to take at least half a day off a week to avoid burning out. So I am hoping to get either Sunday or Saturday off of just relaxing and recuperating. I have put everything on my calendar and it is just a matter of sticking to the schedule.

    Strength and Limitations

    Strength Limitation
    Throughout the foundations, being self-sufficient has proven to be a strength for me. I got through the foundations trying to be as less dependent on anyone as possible and I believe I have learned and understood a lot of the concepts through that. Biggest limitation for me would be my lack of motivation to work with teams or other people.
    Another strength that has solidified throughout foundations is my obsessiveness. I managed to find answers to questions because I wouldn't stop until I did. One limitation that might hinder me is self-doubt. Sometimes I do get an overwhelming feeling of self-doubt and think that I can't do it or this isn't for me. It takes me a while to convince myself otherwise and it does get me down.

    What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

    My biggest non-technical challenge would probably be working in groups or in pairs. I'm an introvert and it takes a lot of effort for me to be around other people. My energy gets drained after a few hours of interpersonal interaction and would always need alone time to recuperate. So I'm a bit worried about spending hours of intense work in a group setting. I also work and think well alone. But hopefully, I ghet to better myself at this through the bootcamp.

    What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

    I really want to work on my ability to work with groups. Don't get me wrong, I love social interactions. In fact, I do believe that I have good bangter and can carry myself in social settings but in saying that, I do really prefer keeping to myself especially when it comes to work. I perform better when working alone but I know working with teams is a necessity in any industry so I want to improve in that aspect.

    What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

    I expect great organization. If the team is organized, it makes me feel like I'm on top of things as well.

    What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

    I expect myself to work hard. No questions asked. I do have somewhat a system when asking for help. I will try to exhaust all my efforts into solving the problem first and if that doesn't work I will have a look if I have an upcoming check in with a facilitator and if I do, I plan to ask my question then because then that gives me more time to solve the problem on my own. Most of the time, I solve it before I even get to chat with the facilitator so that's good. If not, then have a chat with the facilitator.
