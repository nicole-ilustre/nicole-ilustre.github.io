    Learning Javascript

    What did you notice about your learning style? What resources worked for you and why? I noticed structure is really important for me and I do have the urge to tick everything on the checklist which can be a good thing and a bad thing at the same time. A good mixture of visual (video and reading) and hands-on (practice coding) is the best for me. I easily get distracted and bored if I stick to one type for a long time.
    Did you keep within the timebox suggestions? If not, was it due to difficulty understanding the basics, or a feeling like you had to _master_ the basics? Definitely the feeling of having to master the basics! There were a few times when I did not completely grasp the idea and I spent so much time researching and trying to figure it out. It was difficult for me to convince myself to just take what I understand and move on because most of the time things are explained further and some concepts are easier to grasp the more we get into the lesson. But I always cling on to the idea that I have to get this now.

    Devtools Tutorials

    What did you notice about your learning style, strengths, and limitations when doing this crash course? I noticed that I had to mix things up frequently, go from one topic to another. This way I am keeping it fresh all the time. The down side of it though is not getting in a flow. I feel like I'm not giving myself a chance to get in the flow in one subject before jumping to another. I have to find a balance between both.
    Did any blocks come up for you? How did you overcome them? There were a few times where I had to stop and go to another topic as I was just not getting the current one I am studying. The DOM is quite new to me so I had to understand the very basics of it.
